Dr. Handong Yao

Bio

Dr. Handong Yao is an Assistant Professor in the School of Environmental, Civil, Agricultural and Mechanical Engineering (ECAM) at the University of Georgia. Dr. Yao leads the Mobility Lab. His research focuses on developing cyber-physical transportation systems powered by smart infrastructure technologies, including advanced sensing, communication, and automation. He leverages machine learning techniques and traffic flow theory to enhance transportation system sustainability, safety, and efficiency.

Projects

  • Tribal & Rural Autonomous Vehicles for Efficiency, Livability and Safety (TRAVELS), USDOT: developing and deploying autonomous vehicles to enhance safety and efficiency in rural areas.
  • Rural Road Infrastructure Assessment and Enhancement for Autonomous Vehicle Deployment, USDOT CR2C2: collecting multimodal sensing data to evaluate infrastructure readiness for autonomous vehicles in rural areas.
  • Creating a Ten-Year Traffic Safety Infrastructure Plan for the University of Georgia, USDOT: reviewing current studies and practices, deploying safety infrastructure to collect traffic safety data, and analyzing traffic safety data to enhance safety.
  • Realistic Autonomous Vehicle Behavior Investigation for Stakeholder Empowerment (RAISE), FHWA: modeling and simulating the interactions between autonomous vehicles and traffic signals/signs.
