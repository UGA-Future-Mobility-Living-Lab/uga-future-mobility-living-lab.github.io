Dr. Ramviyas Nattanmai Parasuraman

Bio

Dr Ramviyas Nattanmai Parasuraman is an Assistant Professor in the School of Computing at the University of Georgia. He directs the Heterogeneous Robotics Research Lab (HeRoLab), which conducts cutting-edge research in heterogeneous robotics and multi-robot systems, aligned with research areas in collective swarm intelligence, connected vehicles, rescue robotics, and human-robot interfaces. Previously, he had held postdoctoral appointments at Purdue University, USA, and KTH Royal Institute of Technology, Sweden. He conducted his doctoral research at CERN, Switzerland, and obtained his Ph.D. in Robotics and Automation from Universidad Politecnica de Madrid, Spain. Dr. Parasuraman is a recipient of an outstanding Faculty Mentoring Award and the prestigious Marie Sklodowska Curie Early-career Research Fellowship. He has published more than 60 papers in high-impact publication venues in Robotics and Sensor Networking, such as IEEE Transactions on Robotics, Robotics and Automation Letters, Internet of Things, and SMC. He has served as an associate editor for top-tier robotics conferences, including ICRA and IROS.

Projects

  • Dr. Ramviyas Parasuraman (PI) leads a project on "Cooperative Multi-Agent Systems" (2023-2026) sponsored by the U.S. Army Research Lab. The project will explore fundamental research in enabling autonomous robots and AI agents to form a cooperative team with robust localization, decision-making, and learning algorithms under adversarial scenarios. The project outcomes will accelerate innovations supporting the advancements in robotics and autonomous vehicles.
  • Dr. Ramviyas Parasuraman (PI) leads a collaborative research project on "AI-driven Cyber-Physical Heterogeneous Systems for Precision Poultry Farming" (2024-2027) sponsored by USDA. This project develops an integrated Cyber-Physical System (CPS) combining robotics, AI, and advanced sensing to optimize poultry welfare and productivity. Key innovations include continuous vehicle localization, AI-driven anomaly detection, and automated task execution, enhancing farm efficiency, worker safety, and sustainability while advancing smart farming technologies. The research outcomes offer insights into remedying challenges surrounding the application of autonomous vehicles.
