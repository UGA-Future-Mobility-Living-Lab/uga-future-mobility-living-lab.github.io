Dr. Xiaobai Angela Yao

Bio

Dr. Xiaobai Angela Yao is a Professor of Geography at the University of Georgia, where she has been a faculty member since 2002. With a career spanning over two decades, Dr. Yao has made significant contributions at the intersection of GIScience, human mobility, transportation modeling, and machine learning/AI. Her research focuses on human dynamics and public health, integrating cutting-edge AI techniques with geospatial analytics. Angela holds a Ph.D. in Geography from the University at Buffalo, State University of New York, an M.S. in GIS and Urban Analysis from ITC (now Twente University), Netherlands, and a B.S. in GIS and Urban Planning from Wuhan University, China. She also held visiting positions at leading global institutions, including the University of Vienna, Tsinghua University, and the Technical University of Munich.


Dr. Yao has published extensively. Her interdisciplinary and globally collaborative work continues to advance the understanding of human mobility and its impact on the environment and public health. Beyond research, Angela actively contributes to the academic community. She has served as Chair of the International Cartographic Association (ICA) Commission on Geospatial Analysis and Modeling (2015–2023) and as President of the Cartography and Geographic Information Society (CaGIS) (2023), among other leadership positions.
